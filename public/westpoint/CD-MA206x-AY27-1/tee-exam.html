Problem 1 — Data Fundamentals & Probability (30 points)

SLOs: 2, 3, 7

A military police unit tracks the number of traffic violations at a base gate. Over the past year, they observe an average of 5 violations per day. Additionally, the speed of vehicles entering the gate is approximately normally distributed with mean 22 mph and standard deviation 4 mph. The posted speed limit is 15 mph.

(a) (5 pts) What is the probability of observing exactly 3 violations on a given day? Identify the distribution, state its parameter, and show your work.

(b) (5 pts) What is the probability of observing 8 or more violations on a given day? Use the complement.

(c) (5 pts) What proportion of vehicles enter the gate exceeding 28 mph? Show the standardization step and sketch the curve.

(d) (5 pts) The gate commander wants to set a “threshold speed” such that only 5% of vehicles exceed it. What speed should the threshold be?

(e) (5 pts) A random sample of 40 vehicles has a mean entry speed of \(\bar{x} = 23.1\) mph. What is the probability that the sample mean exceeds 23.1 mph? Use the CLT and state your assumptions.

(f) (5 pts) Does your answer to (e) suggest the true mean speed may be higher than 22 mph? Explain your reasoning in 1–2 sentences.

Problem 2 — Conditional Probability & Bayes’ Theorem (30 points)

SLOs: 6, 7

The Army uses a diagnostic test to screen soldiers for a particular medical condition. The condition affects 2% of the active-duty population. The test has the following characteristics:

  • Sensitivity (true positive rate): \(P(\text{Positive} | \text{Has condition}) = 0.95\)
  • Specificity (true negative rate): \(P(\text{Negative} | \text{No condition}) = 0.90\)

(a) (5 pts) Construct a tree diagram showing the probabilities for condition status and test result.

(b) (8 pts) A soldier tests positive. What is the probability they actually have the condition? Use Bayes’ Theorem and show all work.

(c) (5 pts) Interpret your answer to (b). Is this test effective as a standalone diagnostic tool? Explain.

(d) (7 pts) The Army considers requiring a second independent test for everyone who tests positive on the first test. Assuming the second test has the same sensitivity and specificity, what is the probability a soldier has the condition given they test positive on both tests?

(e) (5 pts) Is this two-test procedure a better screening strategy? What role does the base rate (2%) play in the reliability of the test?

Problem 3 — Discrete Random Variables (25 points)

SLOs: 3, 7

An intelligence analyst monitors satellite communication channels. Each channel independently has a 0.15 probability of carrying a signal of interest during a given time window. The analyst monitors 20 channels simultaneously.

(a) (5 pts) Let \(X\) = the number of channels carrying a signal of interest. Identify the distribution, state parameters, and verify conditions.

(b) (5 pts) What is the probability that exactly 4 channels carry a signal of interest?

(c) (5 pts) What is the probability that at least 2 channels carry a signal of interest?

(d) (5 pts) Compute \(E(X)\) and \(\text{SD}(X)\). Interpret \(E(X)\) in context.

(e) (5 pts) If the analyst monitors for 3 independent time windows, what is the probability that none of the 3 windows has exactly 0 channels of interest? (Hint: first find the probability of 0 channels in one window.)

Problem 4 — Confidence Intervals (35 points)

SLOs: 1, 5, 6

A logistics officer wants to estimate the average fuel consumption (gallons per mile) of a fleet of tactical vehicles. She randomly selects 28 vehicles and records their fuel consumption over a standardized route. The data yields \(\bar{x} = 0.42\) gallons/mile and \(s = 0.08\) gallons/mile.

(a) (5 pts) State and verify the conditions for constructing a confidence interval for the true mean fuel consumption.

(b) (10 pts) Construct a 90% confidence interval for \(\mu\). Show all work including the critical value and margin of error.

(c) (7 pts) Interpret the confidence interval in context. Use precise language.

(d) (5 pts) The fleet manual states the expected fuel consumption is 0.38 gallons/mile. Based on your CI, is there evidence that the fleet is consuming more fuel than expected? Explain without conducting a formal hypothesis test.

(e) (8 pts) In a separate study, the officer samples 200 vehicles and wants to estimate the proportion that exceed 0.50 gallons/mile. She finds 34 vehicles exceed this threshold. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion. State the conditions.

Problem 5 — One-Sample Hypothesis Test (30 points)

SLOs: 1, 5, 6

An Army dietitian claims that the average daily caloric intake of cadets during summer training exceeds the recommended 3,200 calories. A random sample of 20 cadets during CBT yields \(\bar{x} = 3,450\) calories and \(s = 380\) calories.

(a) (5 pts) State the hypotheses in symbols and words. Identify this as one-tailed or two-tailed.

(b) (5 pts) State and verify the conditions. What additional assumption is required given the sample size?

(c) (8 pts) Compute the test statistic and find the p-value using R-Lite. Show all work.

(d) (5 pts) At \(\alpha = 0.01\), state your decision and write a conclusion in context.

(e) (7 pts) Compute a 99% confidence interval for \(\mu\). Is it consistent with your test result? Explain the connection.

Problem 6 — Two-Sample t-Test (35 points)

SLOs: 1, 3, 5, 6

VR Training Traditional
\(n\) 24 30
\(\bar{x}\) 87.2 83.5
\(s\) 5.1 6.9

(a) (5 pts) State the hypotheses. Justify your choice of one-tailed or two-tailed.

(b) (5 pts) State and verify the conditions. Should you use pooled or Welch? Justify.

Problem 8 — Two-Proportion z-Test (30 points)

SLOs: 1, 5, 6

A study compares the injury rates during field training between two brigades. In Brigade A, 28 out of 350 soldiers sustained injuries. In Brigade B, 42 out of 400 soldiers sustained injuries.

(a) (5 pts) State the hypotheses for testing whether the injury rates differ between the two brigades.

(b) (5 pts) Verify the conditions for a two-proportion z-test.

(c) (8 pts) Compute the pooled proportion, the test statistic, and the p-value.

(d) (5 pts) At \(\alpha = 0.05\), state your decision and conclusion in context.

(e) (7 pts) Construct a 95% confidence interval for \(p_A - p_B\) using the unpooled formula. Does it contain 0? Interpret the interval and connect it to your hypothesis test result.
