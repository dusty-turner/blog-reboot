TipCoverage Notes
  • SLO 4 (statistical models / software) is meaningfully assessed for the first time on the TEE via Problems 9 and 10. This fills the gap identified in the WPR I and WPR II SLO maps.
  • SLO 6 has the broadest coverage — every problem has elements of critical evaluation (conditions, interpretation, design awareness).
  • SLO 2 is the lightest at 9%. This is acceptable since graphical displays are better assessed through the project. Problem 9g (residual plot interpretation) also indirectly assesses graph reading.
  • Block III material (100 pts, 29%) is proportional — students have 8–10 lessons of regression/ANOVA content, and this represents roughly a third of the exam.

NoteBalance Assessment

Block II dominates (47%) which is appropriate — it’s the largest block and the conceptual core. Block I (24%) and Block III (29%) are balanced. The TEE-only Block III material is substantial enough to be meaningful but not so heavy that cadets who struggled with regression are doomed.
