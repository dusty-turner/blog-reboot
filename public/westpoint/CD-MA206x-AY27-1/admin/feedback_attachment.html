Comments on FDW 2026 and Workshop Week Drafts

March 2026 | LTC Dusty Turner

1 What I took away from these documents

FDW 2026:

  • The five-week progression from immersion to readiness to practice teaching gives new faculty a clear path.
  • The teaching dimensions provide a shared understanding of what we do, why we do it, and our goals as instructors.
  • The June certification is a smart way to pressure-test the structure before new faculty arrive.

Workshop Week:

  • Course stratification by size with scaled expectations makes sense – large courses need more structure than small ones.
  • Writing down PD/CD roles and responsibilities clarifies expectations.
  • I would recommend against having outgoing CDs or APDs run a “best of” teaching lab during the transition. If the goal of this new structure is to make change - we’ve been asking the old leadership to make change for several semesters now.

2 A note on implementation

I support the goals in both documents. The structure is clear and the intent is right. My one concern is time - senior faculty are already stretched across teaching, research, DACing, MaRS, additional duties, taskings, and commitments external to the department, and this structure asks much more of their time. This will require us to be deliberate about where we invest our energy, clear about priorities, and willing to accept that some tasks will not get the same level of effort. The department needs to find a way to lean more on junior faculty, give them more ownership, and be honest about which competing demands we can scale back or cut so we have the bandwidth to do this well. With that in mind, here is my implementation plan.

3 How I plan to implement this for MA206

Given that so much of MA206 is new, I want the team to start the year with a clear picture of where we’re headed. Here’s how I intend to set that up.

3.1 Summative assessments built before the semester starts

  • I will have all versions of all summative assessments (WPR 1 and WPR 2 and TEE) complete by the end of the summer.
  • The course project will be finalized as well.
  • Each assessment will include a rubric and a document mapping every question back to a student learning objective (SLO).
  • The goal is transparency - instructors will know from day one what expectations we have of students. We’ll walk a fine line between not teaching to the test and ensuring every instructor understands what to emphasize and what cadets will need to demonstrate on exams.

3.2 Teaching Labs (Forges) led by the APD

  • I will run the teaching labs. With this much new material in 206, I think this is especially important.
  • Early on, I will personally lead both the expectations and demonstration portions of each teaching lab. I intend to do this for an extended period – not because others can’t, but because I want to set the standard and demonstrate it week in and week out. When I eventually ask others to lead specific portions, the intent and expectations will be clear because they’ve seen it modeled consistently.
  • Each teaching lab will focus on the next three lessons: the finer points of what we want cadets to know, what they’ll need to show on assessments, and the deeper understanding of the material so that instructors can teach at a deeper level – not just cover content, but teach it well.

3.3 Course Director responsibilities

The CD will own day-to-day course execution, including:

  • WebAssign management
  • Managing the Canvas page
  • Tracking course coverage to ensure sections stay aligned on pace and content
  • Maintaining the course schedule and syllabus
  • Coordinating make-up exams and enforcing late policies
  • Serving as the first line of support for instructor questions on content and grading
  • Providing regular course status updates to the PD - pace, emerging issues, and grade trends

3.4 Weekly team teaching meetings

  • I want to establish weekly team teaching meetings, and I expect the format will evolve as guidance crystallizes.
  • Attendance groupings will be determined by a mix of instructor seniority, experience, and off-hour availability.
  • The purpose: share ideas on what to teach and with demonstration for the next three lessons. Leadership will rotate among instructors, and I will track that rotation.
  • The intent is to build a culture where instructors learn from each other and take ownership of how they teach - not just what they teach.
