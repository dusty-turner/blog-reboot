SLO 4 (statistical models/software) is not directly assessed on either WPR. It relies entirely on the final project and Block III lessons. Consider:

  • Adding a software-output interpretation question to WPR II, or
  • Ensuring the project rubric heavily weights SLO 4

  • Including a graph interpretation component on WPR II (e.g., interpret a boxplot comparison or residual plot)

SLO 3 (variability/randomness) has limited Block II coverage (only L17, L24). The connection between randomness and inference should be made more explicit in L20 (Intro to HT) objectives — consider adding an objective like “Explain the role of randomness in hypothesis testing.”
